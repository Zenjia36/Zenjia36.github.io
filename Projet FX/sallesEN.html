Our partner gyms in Île-de-France

Although Paris is not renowned for its mountains or cliffs, the Paris region makes up for this with a wide choice of indoor climbing gyms. These facilities, with their artificial climbing structures (SAE) and modern equipment, make it possible to practice this activity all year round. With the growing popularity of climbing, which is accessible both indoors and outdoors, in bouldering as well as on routes, here is our selection of the 10 best climbing gyms according to our editors.

Explore climbing gyms near you:

  • Climb Up Porte d’Italie


    • Climb Up Paris-Porte d'Italie is the capital's largest climbing gym, a true meeting place for climbers of all levels. With its 4,000 m² of air-conditioned space in the 13th arrondissement, the gym offers dedicated bouldering and climbing areas, with 11 m-high walls accessible independently or as part of various courses: private lessons, annual training courses, route initiations, children's workshops, and much more.
    Whether you're a beginner or an experienced climber, you'll find what you're looking for, whether in the children's cave, on the routes, in the boulders or on the Spraywall. More than just a climbing area, Climb Up also offers a variety of services: telecommuting (with on-site consumption), catering (snack & bar), training areas, and moments of relaxation with sauna and hammam. Accessible to all, it's the ideal place to surpass your limits, share your passion and enjoy a unique experience.

    Climb Up – Porte d’Italie
    18 Av. de la Porte d’Italie, 75013 Paris
    Open every day, from 8am to midnight.
    More information here!

  • Climb Up – Aubervilliers

    • With 29 gyms throughout France, Climb Up is the country's largest network of climbing gyms! In Aubervilliers, discover Europe's largest climbing gym. This impressive venue invites you to climb two emblematic arches, with a multitude of routes and boulders suitable for everyone, from beginners to seasoned climbers.
    Climb Up Aubervilliers also offers courses for all levels and a unique Fun Climbing area, perfect for kids to have fun and get exercise at the same time. Much more than just a climbing gym, Climb Up Aubervilliers also offers a restaurant, a wellness area with sauna and hammam, and a coworking space. It's the perfect place for climbing enthusiasts to enjoy a friendly, multifunctional setting!

    Climb Up Aubervilliers
    111 avenue Victor Hugo, 93300 Aubervilliers
    Open every day, from 8am to midnight.
    More information here!

  • Arkose Chevaleret

    • Climbing, intrinsically linked to nature, inspires Arkose to make an active commitment to sustainable development. The gym runs exclusively on 100% renewable energy and features a range of environmentally-friendly equipment. To complete the experience, Arkose offers a yoga room ideal for strengthening muscles, as well as a canteen offering tasty, simple dishes made from fresh, local produce.
    For adventure-seekers, Arkose also organizes train escapades to a variety of destinations, enabling you to discover the most beautiful natural outdoor climbing sites. A great way to combine passion and respect for the environment!

    Arkose Chevaleret
    181 Rue du Chevaleret, 75013 Paris
    Open every day, from 7am to midnight.
    More information here!

  • Escalad’Indoor

    • Created in 1990 on the initiative of three PE teachers from the Institut National des Jeunes Sourds, this climbing gym, affiliated to the Fédération Française de la Montagne et de l'Escalade (FFME), came into being thanks to the provision of a wall in the school's gymnasium. From its modest beginnings, the artificial climbing structure (SAE) has grown to reach a height of 10 metres and a width of 45 metres. A second hall has been added, offering a wide variety of areas such as slabs, dihedrals, a roof, as well as a Güllich pan and other modern equipment. Escalad'Indoor doesn't limit itself to indoor climbing: the club organizes outdoor outings to discover natural sites, and offers educational resources such as training sheets and specialized books. All of this in a friendly atmosphere where the transmission of knowledge and the pleasure of climbing are at the heart of our priorities.

    Escalad’Indoor
    Salle : 11 rue Henry Barbusse, 75005 Paris
    SAE: INJS gymnasium
    Open monday, tuesday and thursday, from 5pm30 to 10pm.
    More information here!

  • Hardbloc

    • Just 10 minutes from Paris, discover one of the biggest bouldering gyms in France. With 280 varied routes spread over 8 levels of difficulty, every visit brings a new challenge. This unique venue offers courses for all ages, whether you're a beginner or an experienced climber, in group or private lessons. To complete your experience, enjoy a sauna, a weight room and a friendly bar, perfect for relaxing and recharging your batteries after an intense session!

    Hardbloc
    11 rue Parmentier, 94140 Alfortville
    Open from monday to friday, from 10am30 to 10pm30.
    Le week-end de 10h à 20h
    More information here!

  • Blocbuster

    • Blocbuster, with its three gyms in the west of Paris, is dedicated to bouldering. Combining accessible challenges for beginners and demanding routes for seasoned climbers, these gyms offer weekly group and individual lessons to learn, progress or simply discover this captivating discipline. Each gym has its own identity:

    • Courbevoie, one of the largest in the 92 region, covers 1,200 m².
    • La Défense features an impressive 20-metre-long slab wall.
    • Versailles, the largest of all, includes a special area for children aged 3 and over.

    These unique venues combine sport, conviviality and diversity to satisfy enthusiasts of all ages!

    Blocbuster
    Courbevoie – 47 avenue de l’Europe, 92400 Courbevoie
    La Défense – 48, Square des Corolles, 92400 Courbevoie
    Versailles – 11 rue Exelmans, 78000 Versailles
    More information here!

  • Climbing District

    • Here's an innovative concept that rethinks climbing by blending sport, art and relaxation. These gyms, with their elegant, artistic design, not only offer climbing walls, but also hybrid spaces where you can relax, work on large connected tables, or enjoy a rich program of musical and artistic events.
    On the climbing side, there's a wall with 8 levels of difficulty, classes for all levels, and courses to help you progress. For even more fun, there's an interactive digital wall where you can challenge your friends. And of course, the whole experience is rounded off by a cosy bar and an organic counter to recharge your batteries after exercise. A unique experience where sport and lifestyle meet!

    Climbing District
    Batignolles – 69, rue Mstislav Rostropovitch, 75017 Paris
    Buttes Chaumont – 55, rue de Meaux, 75019 Paris
    Saint-Lazare – 26bis rue de Saint Petersbourg, 75008 Paris
    Pont-de-Neuilly – 166 avenue Charles de Gaulle, 92200 Neuilly-sur-Seine
    Open every day.
    More information here!

  • Antrebloc

    • Antrebloc stands out with its three complementary areas: AntreBloc, AntreForme, and AntrePotes. Together, they offer a wide range of activities: bouldering, route climbing, tunnel climbing, moonboarding, as well as crossfit, cardio, boxing and relaxation with a sauna. And to extend the experience, a friendly bar awaits you for a well-deserved after-party.
    Climbers will appreciate the 50 or so routes, which are regularly renewed to guarantee new challenges on every visit. Founded in 1998, Antrebloc is an emblematic place where sport and conviviality meet in a warm atmosphere.

    Antrebloc
    5 rue Henri Barbusse, 94800 Villejuif
    Open from monday to friday, from 11am to 11pm30.
    During weekends from 9am to 9pm.
