FAQ

Got questions? Here are the answers to the most frequently asked questions:

  • What equipment is required? Climbing shoes and a harness if you want to climb a route, but you can usually rent both from the gym if you don't have your own. Otherwise, comfortable clothing and water to stay hydrated.

  • Is bouldering dangerous? Not if you respect security rules.

  • Is there a minimum age? Most gyms accept children aged 6 and over.

  • How much does bouldering cost? The cost of climbing can vary according to several factors, including the type of climbing (bouldering, route, outdoor), location, and whether you choose a climbing gym or natural sites.
    For occasional beginners: expect to pay between €15 and €30 per session (including admission and equipment hire).
    For regular practice: around €500 to €1,000 per year, including subscription and purchase of basic equipment.
    If you're a beginner, it's often a good idea to start with rental equipment before investing in your own.

  • What are the benefits of climbing? Climbing is a complete discipline that combines sport, reflection and self-improvement. It's an ideal activity for anyone looking to improve their physical condition while cultivating a strong mental outlook. Whether you're a beginner or an expert, every session is an opportunity to progress both physically and mentally!

  • What are the difficulty levels? Bouldering, practiced on shorter structures without ropes, has its own rating systems. The French system uses the following dues:
    • Ratings: 1 to 9, often subdivided (e.g. 6A, 6A+, 6B).
    • More accessible than the route ratings, but the technical difficulty of the movements is accentuated.
    • Example :
    • 1 to 3: Very easy, ideal for beginners.
    • 4 to 5: Intermediate, requires good basic technique.
    • 6A to 7C: Advanced, requiring strength and precision.
    • 8A and up: Expert, complex and very physical movements.
    • The level of difficulty is often represented by a color code on the holds. This depends on the climbing gym.
